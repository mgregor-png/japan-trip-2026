# Japan with Kids — Practical Tips

Sources: bontraveler.com, thetokyochapter.com, flyertalk.com, user notes

## Logistics
- **Fly into Haneda** (not Narita) — much closer to central Tokyo, huge difference with tired toddlers
- **Takkyubin luggage forwarding** — ship bags hotel-to-hotel for ~1500 JPY/bag (~350 Kc). Available at any konbini or hotel front desk. Single biggest QoL upgrade for families.
- **Overnight in Tokyo before departure** — never try to fly out same day from Osaka/Onomichi. Always buffer a night.
- **JR Pass may not be worth it** — calculate individual tickets vs pass for actual legs. Post-2023 price hike + Nozomi restriction makes it marginal.

## Accommodation
- **Mimaru apartments** — family-oriented chain with kitchenettes, coin laundry, floor space. Locations in Tokyo, Osaka. Top recommendation across multiple sources.
- **&Here** — another apartment-hotel brand good for families
- **Ryokan** — look for private family baths (kashikiri-buro/kazoku-buro). Most public onsen don't allow diapered children. 1-2 nights is ideal, not more.

## Diapers & Baby Supplies
- Google Maps: search "Akachanhompo near me", "Nishimatsuya near me", or "Toys R Us near me"
- **National Azabu** (Hiroo, Tokyo) delivers Western groceries nationwide

## Food with Kids
- **Conveyor belt sushi** (Uobei, kura sushi) — kids pick visually, no pressure, many non-fish options
- **Interactive dining** is the move: shabu shabu, okonomiyaki, teppanyaki — kids engaged, no waiting
- **All-you-can-eat shabu shabu** (Momo Paradise, Syabu-yo, Nabezu) eliminates decision fatigue
- **Royal Host** chain — omurice is universally kid-approved fallback
- Use **Tabelog** (not Google Maps) to find family-friendly restaurants. Ask for "okosama" (children's) meals.
- Reserve at least half of dinner restaurants a few days in advance

## Activities — Tokyo
- **TeamLab Borderless/Planets** — interactive digital art, great for 5yo
- **Fukagawa Edo Museum** — reconstructed indoor Edo village, hands-on, rarely on tourist lists
- **Tokyo Toy Museum** (Shinjuku) — wooden toys, interactive play
- **Round1** — rainy day gold. Arcade, bowling, karaoke, sports under one roof.
- **Inokashira Park** — pedal boats, playground, zoo
- **Komazawa Park** — massive playground area

## Activities — Mt Fuji
- Don't plan a whole day around "seeing" Fuji — 66% chance of cloud cover
- Best family viewing spots: Fujiyoshida, Lake Kawaguchiko
- Doable as a day trip from Tokyo (~2h by bus/train)

## Activities — Osaka
- **NIFREL** (Expo Park) — modern interactive aquarium/zoo, toddler-perfect
- **Nara deer park** — peak toddler content, 45min from Osaka

## General Rules
- Leave time at least once a day for free play (playgrounds, parks, play centers)
- 3-4 night stays minimum per base — no city-hopping with toddlers
- Hit restrooms at shrines/parks BEFORE walking crowded commercial streets
- Avoid Golden Week (late Apr-May) and Obon (mid-Aug)
