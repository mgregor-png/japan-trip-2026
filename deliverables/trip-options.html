Onomichi

4-5 nights
Shinkansen → Fukuyama (1h 20min) + local JR (20min)
Tiny Inland Sea harbor town. Flat waterfront shotengai for stroller walks, harbor cats, legendary Onomichi ramen. Slow pace, local life. Day trips to Tomonoura (Ponyo's fishing village) and Miyajima (floating torii + deer).
Inland Sea views Onomichi ramen Tomonoura (Ponyo village) Miyajima day trip Harbor cats

Day Trips

Nara From Osaka · 45min train
Deer park (hundreds of friendly deer), Todai-ji temple (giant Buddha). Peak toddler content. Free-roaming deer eat from your hand. Flat, stroller-friendly.
Mt Fuji / Kawaguchiko From Tokyo · 2h bus
Don't "go to Fuji" — pick a beautiful viewing spot. Lake Kawaguchiko has playgrounds, boat rides, and (weather permitting) stunning Fuji backdrop. 66% chance of clouds though.
Miyajima Island From Onomichi · 1.5h train+ferry
Floating torii gate, friendly deer, flat waterfront walk. Very stroller-doable along main route. One of the trip highlights for any age.
Tomonoura From Onomichi · 40min train+bus
Tiny fishing port, inspiration for Ponyo. Extremely photogenic, quiet. Flat harbor area, cats, small beaches. Very few services — bring snacks.

Day Trips

Nara From Osaka · 45min train
Deer park, Todai-ji temple. Peak toddler content. Flat, stroller-friendly. Bring deer crackers.
Mt Fuji / Kawaguchiko From Tokyo · 2h bus
Lake views, playgrounds, boat rides. Weather dependent (66% cloud chance). Worth a shot if forecast is clear.
Churaumi Aquarium + Emerald Beach From Chatan · 1.5h drive north
One of the world's best aquariums — whale shark tank is jaw-dropping. Emerald Beach next door is gorgeous. Full day trip, pack swimsuits.
Naha / Makishi Market From Chatan · 30min drive
Kokusai Street, Makishi Public Market (pick your seafood, they cook it upstairs). Tsuboya pottery district. Shuri Castle grounds.
Yomitan / Zanpa Cape From Chatan · 20min drive
Pottery village (Yachimun no Sato), lighthouse, tide pools. Local, quiet. Kids love the rocky coast.

🧳 Luggage

  • Takkyubin — ship bags between hotels for ~350 Kc/bag. Available at any konbini or hotel front desk. Travel light on shinkansen.
  • Fly into Haneda, not Narita — significantly closer to central Tokyo with exhausted toddlers
  • Always overnight in Tokyo before departure — never try to fly out same day from Osaka

🏠 Accommodation

  • Mimaru apartments — family-oriented chain with kitchenettes, coin laundry, floor space. Locations in Tokyo + Osaka.
  • &Here — another good apartment-hotel brand for families
  • Ryokan: look for kashikiri-buro (private family bath). Most public onsen don't allow diapered children. 1-2 nights max.

🍱 Food with Kids

  • Conveyor belt sushi (Uobei, Kura Sushi) — kids pick visually, many non-fish options, no pressure
  • Interactive dining is the move: shabu shabu, okonomiyaki, teppanyaki — kids engaged, no waiting
  • Use Tabelog (not Google Maps) to find family restaurants. Ask for "okosama" (children's) meals.
  • Reserve at least half of dinners a few days in advance
  • Royal Host chain — omurice is the universal kid-approved fallback
Must-try formats: tempura, sukiyaki/shabu-shabu, sushi, izakaya, okonomiyaki, teppanyaki

🎮 Activities

  • TeamLab — interactive digital art installations. Multiple locations across Japan.
  • Fukagawa Edo Museum (Tokyo) — reconstructed indoor Edo village, hands-on, rarely on tourist lists
  • Round1 — rainy day gold. Arcade, bowling, karaoke, sports under one roof. Nationwide.
  • Leave time at least once daily for free play — playgrounds, parks, play centers
  • Japanese zoos tend to disappoint vs Western standards — skip them

🗻 Mt Fuji

  • Don't plan a whole day around "seeing" Fuji — 66% chance of cloud cover
  • Best family spots: Fujiyoshida, Lake Kawaguchiko (playgrounds, boat rides, Fuji backdrop)
  • Doable as a day trip from Tokyo (~2h by bus)
  • Go if the forecast is clear, have a backup plan if not

👶 Baby/Toddler Supplies

  • Diapers: Google Maps → "Akachanhompo near me", "Nishimatsuya near me", or "Toys R Us near me"
  • National Azabu (Hiroo, Tokyo) delivers Western groceries nationwide
  • Hotel pools are rare and mostly only open Jul-Aug — don't count on pool time
  • Most playgrounds have separate toddler sections with age-appropriate equipment

🚄 Transport

  • JR Pass: calculate before buying — post-2023 price hike makes it marginal for some routes
  • Kids under 6 ride JR free (unreserved seats only)
  • Hit restrooms at shrines/parks BEFORE walking crowded commercial streets
  • Suica/Pasmo IC cards work on virtually all urban transit

🛍️ Souvenirs

  • Sou Sou brand (Tokyo + Kyoto) — kids' yukata with built-in straps, jinbei pajamas. Practical souvenirs that actually get worn at home.
  • Konbini snacks make great gifts — stock up at 7-Eleven, Lawson, FamilyMart
